2. Methodology
2.1. Framework of risk perception and management
Fig. 1 displayed the framework concerning on risk evaluation and management of excavation engineering. The risk evaluation and risk management are different from each other. Take this study as an example, a cloud model-based approach is developed and utilized to perceive the risk level of excavation system. It is risk evaluation. Countermeasures are adopted based on the results of risk analysis, which is the risk management. As seen in Fig. 1, there are two databases, namely project database and measurement database. The project database mainly is the collection of material concerning on project, such as geological conditions, construction scheme, designs and construction drawings. Geological conditions are the significant section for underground space engineering. Different stratums pose various requirements on construction technologies. The information for geological conditions is obtained from geological investigation report. In this report, measured data concerning on soil parameters are obtained. The recommended soil parameters aided in the design of underground apace structures. Construction scheme are the detailed steps for the implementation of construction technology since there are sequence of procedures on excavation construction. The design and construction drawings provide the views of underground structures in different perspectives. As for the measured database, it mainly includes the measured data for influential factors on excavation engineering, such as lateral wall deflection, ground surface settlement, support axial force. There are many monitoring equipment devices, such as total station, electronic level and oblique tube, which are used to monitor the response of excavation during construction. Moreover, the onsite engineers are required to take note of construction situation of engineering. The multi-source data from project and measured database help to establish the risk status evaluation approach concerning on excavation engineering. The risk status perceptions for excavation system were implemented via the application of developed approach. Risk evaluation results provide the reference for project managers taking corresponded risk mitigation measures, which mainly includes emergency measures, strengthen monitored frequencies of influential factors.
2.2. Risk status evaluation approach for excavation system
Developed approach comprises three phases: multi-sources information collection, development of the benchmark cloud model (BCM) and identified cloud model (ICM) and calculating the similar membership degree between BCM and ICM to determine the risk status level of excavation systems.
Phase 1: Multi-sources information collection. In this phase, the influential factors should be determined based on the engineering practice, previous case histories. The measured data from multi-sources (geotechnical investigation report, safety construction record, filed inspections) are collected. For instance, data for geological factors were accessed from the geological investigation report, while data for measured items were accessed from monitored devices. The other factor, such as dynamic load near excavation, is required construction engineer to provide the score according to construction situation of excavation. These measured data for influential factors are directly input to the developed approach.
Phase 2: Construct the BCM and ICM. The BCM is built based on the engineering practice. In addition, the number of cloud drops and type of cloud generator were defined. ICM is obtained based on the developed approach via multi-source information aggregation. In this process, the variables are assumed as normal distribution, which is the basic assumption for cloud model. The two stages weights determination is developed to fuse the information from factors. Exij, Enij and Heij of the corresponding influential factors in all risk status levels were synthesised to obtain ICM.
Phase 3: Determine risk levels of excavation systems. Similar membership degree between the BCM and ICM was calculated. The level with the highest normalized similar membership degree was assigned as the risk level of excavation system.
Risk status evaluation approach is developed based on cloud model. Descriptions about cloud model is presented in Appendix. A in the supplementary materials. The two stage weights determination method is introduced to aggregate the multi-sources information from excavation system. In the cloud model, there are two kinds of cloud generator, named normal forward cloud generator and normal backward cloud generator.
